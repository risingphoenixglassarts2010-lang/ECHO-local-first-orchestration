ECHO & The Brain: How This Project Supports People Living With Cognitive Challenges
A simple explanation

Why I Built This
After my traumatic brain injury, my mind didn’t work the way it used to.

I struggled with things like:

keeping track of what I was doing

remembering the next step

switching tasks without losing the thread

staying organized when I got tired

holding onto context when life got overwhelming

If you live with TBI, ADHD, autism, chronic fatigue, PTSD, or burnout, you probably know exactly what that feels like.

I wasn’t trying to build a “tech project.”
I was trying to stay coherent.

ECHO grew out of that need.

It’s not a medical tool.
It’s not a treatment.
It’s not a cure.

It’s simply a structure — a kind of cognitive scaffolding — that helps me keep my thoughts steady when my brain gets overloaded.

What ECHO Helps With
ECHO is designed to support the kinds of challenges many people with brain‑related conditions face every day:

1. Losing track of what you were doing
ECHO helps keep your place so you don’t have to hold everything in your head.

2. Getting overwhelmed easily
It breaks things into smaller, calmer steps.

3. Forgetting context
It keeps the “big picture” steady so you don’t have to rebuild it every time.

4. Struggling with executive function
It gives you a predictable structure to lean on when your brain is tired.

5. Mental fatigue
It reduces the amount of energy needed to stay organized.

6. Dropped threads
It remembers the things your brain lets go of when you’re overloaded.

Why This Matters for TBI & Neurodivergent Communities
People living with TBI, ADHD, autism, chronic fatigue, and similar conditions often deal with:

brain fog

memory gaps

difficulty switching tasks

trouble planning

overwhelm

fatigue that shuts everything down

losing continuity from day to day

ECHO was built to support exactly those moments.

It doesn’t replace your thinking.
It doesn’t make decisions for you.
It doesn’t act like an “AI agent.”

It simply holds the structure steady so you don’t have to fight your brain to stay on track.

Think of it like a cognitive handrail.

What ECHO Is NOT
To keep things clear and safe:

It is not medical advice

It is not a therapy

It is not a diagnosis

It is not a cure

It is not a replacement for professional care

It’s just a tool I built for myself — and I’m sharing it in case it helps someone else.

Why I Believe This Matters
My brother‑in‑law spent years running Hinds’ Feet Farm, a nonprofit supporting people with traumatic brain injuries. I saw firsthand how much structure, support, and 

cognitive scaffolding people need after a brain injury.

I never expected to need that kind of support myself.

But when I did, I built the tool I couldn’t find anywhere else.

ECHO is my way of giving back — to the TBI community, to neurodivergent folks, and to anyone who knows what it feels like when your brain can’t keep up with your life.
