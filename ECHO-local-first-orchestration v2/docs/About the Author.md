About the Author

Michael Burger is a systems‑oriented integrator with nearly three decades of experience in high‑tolerance fabrication, industrial electrical work, access‑control infrastructure,

and reliability‑focused production environments. His career has centered on diagnosing failure modes, extending system lifespan, and designing durable, field‑ready solutions under 

real‑world constraints.

ECHO emerged from this same engineering mindset. During a period of constrained cognitive bandwidth, Michael recognized the need for a framework that could provide structure,

continuity, and stability under load — a cognitive exoskeleton capable of supporting clear reasoning when complexity or pressure threatened to overwhelm.

The architecture that followed reflects the environments he has spent his life working in:

safety‑first

deterministic

resilient

boundary‑aware

designed to hold up under stress

Michael approaches AI not as a model researcher, but as a systems builder. His work focuses on orchestration, boundaries, and reliability — the parts of AI that must function 

predictably if intelligence is going to be useful in the real world.

ECHO is the formalization of those principles into an open‑source architecture designed to support human agency, stabilize cognition, and turn repeated reasoning into durable structure.
