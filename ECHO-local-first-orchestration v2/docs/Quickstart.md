Quickstart Guide
Welcome to ECHO — a local‑first cognitive architecture designed to safely mediate LLM intent through deterministic, human‑directed workflows.
This guide provides a high‑level introduction to how ECHO works, how to think about its structure, and how to explore the repository.

ECHO is designed to be understood from the outside without exposing any internal logic or protected mechanisms.

What ECHO Is
ECHO is a seven‑layer cognitive exoskeleton that wraps around an LLM to provide:

Identity stabilization  
Ensures consistent behavior across time and prevents persona drift.

Directional alignment  
Keeps the system anchored to human intent and doctrine.

Preserved human agency  
ECHO never initiates actions or goals on its own.

Deterministic execution  
Plans are structured, predictable, and reproducible.

Drift and hallucination prevention  
Guardrails ensure coherence and safety at every step.

ECHO is not an agent, not autonomous, and not self‑directed.
It is a cognitive scaffold that strengthens human thinking while remaining fully under human control.

How It Works (High‑Level Runtime Flow)
ECHO operates through a disciplined, layered pipeline:

1. You provide intent
Every cycle begins with explicit human direction.
ECHO never assumes goals or acts independently.

2. Interpreter — Aligns with Doctrine
The Interpreter Layer stabilizes identity and ensures all reasoning aligns with the system’s philosophical framing.

3. State — Provides continuity
Compressed memory and context anchors maintain coherence without storing raw transcripts.

4. HUD — Highlights what matters
The cognitive overlay filters noise, surfaces salience, and clarifies what the user is actually asking.

5. Execution — Structures the plan
Intent becomes a deterministic workflow, broken into clear, safe, reproducible steps.

6. Integrity — Ensures safety and coherence
Outputs are checked for alignment, consistency, and adherence to doctrine.

7. Evolution — Refines the system over time
Long‑term improvements occur slowly and intentionally, never through autonomous learning.

This flow ensures predictable, stable, human‑directed behavior.

Why Local‑First?
ECHO is intentionally designed to run locally because:

Privacy is absolute  
No cloud logging, no external observers, no data leakage.

Determinism requires local control  
Cloud models change; local models stay pinned and predictable.

Identity must remain stable  
A cognitive exoskeleton cannot rely on shifting cloud personalities.

Offline capability increases reliability  
ECHO works anywhere, anytime, without network dependency.

Human sovereignty is preserved  
The user owns the environment, the model, and the boundaries.

Local‑first is not a technical preference — it is a philosophical stance.
